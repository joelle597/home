Good morning!

The ASRJC ICT Champions programme is an initiative at Anderson Serangoon Junior College in Singapore, aimed at enhancing digital literacy among students. The programme provides students with opportunities to develop and apply their ICT skills through various activities and projects. ICT Champions act as peer leaders, guiding their schoolmates and participating in events that promote digital literacy skills across the college community. Generative AI for Everyone

This course covers the fundamentals of generative AI, including its capabilities and limitations, with hands-on exercises that teach practical applications for everyday tasks and tips for effective prompt engineering. Students will explore advanced AI uses beyond basic prompting, gain experience with generative AI tools, and understand common real-world applications. The course also provides insights into the broader impact of AI on business and society.

CS50 Introduction to Computer Science

Harvard University's CS50 is an introductory computer science course designed for students with or without programming experience. It focuses on problem solving with an emphasis on correctness, design, and style, covering topics such as computational thinking, abstraction, algorithms, and data structures. The course starts with C to build foundational programming skills and an understanding of how computers work, then progresses to Python, SQL, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for practical applications, including web and mobile app development. It concludes with a final project that allows students to apply their skills in a creative and computational way.
